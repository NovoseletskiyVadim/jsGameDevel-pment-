Regulations:

The field represents 19x19 lines. Propulsion opponents make stones (chips) to the points of intersection of the lines(a1 ... s19) ( img.1 ). Doing the move the player places one stone on the board in any un occupied position point of intersection of lines (intersection is called points). Stones placed on the board, do not move, but may be captured by the enemy and taken away from it.

Each stone must have at least one lady (point of freedom, respiratory point) - adjacent vertically or horizontally (but not diagonally!) unoccupied points ( img.2 ). Neighboring or linked by a continuous chain of neighbors is a stones of one the colors form a group and divide the lady among themselves (that is, any lady of any one the stone in the group refers simultaneously to all the rocks of this group). When a stone or a group of stones is surrounded by rivals so, that she does not have points of freedom, she is considered captured and removed from the board.

A player may refuse another turn by saying "pass". If the opponent also makes a "pass", the game is considered complete, but it is done counting according to the rules.

The "Go" rules are simple, but simplicity ends where it starts count points The main source of discrepancies is the question of what should be considered "points of victory"? By and large, there are two approaches: Japanese (counting territory + captive stones) and Chinese (calculation of the seized area).

According to Japanese rules, captive stones are removed from the board and move on the territory of the same color. Then the territory transformed for ease of counting and counting the territory one and another color.

According to Chinese rules captives are removed from the board and return to the bowl for the stones. Then they are calculated stones, exhibited on the board and the territory of only one color, adding stones from the bowls and transforming the territory for convenience of counting. If the amount of stones and territory is equal or more than 181, then this party won the party. We deduct from the sum 181, double, add 1 and get the value of the prize.

AGA applies both the one and the other system - with the consent. If consent is not achieved, then use the Japanese system. The result for both counting systems usually coincides (within one point). In order to achieve a complete match according to AGA rules, the last move should be do white
